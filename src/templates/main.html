The Java Sea was the scene of many naval battles over history, the most recent ones during the second world war.

Over one hundred ships sank there between 1941 and 1945. They lay at the bottom of the sea at depths reaching 80m.

In recent years, many of those shipwrecks have been mysteriously and illegally salvaged for metal, and governments fear others are at risk.

Wreck sites are often considered war graves and should not be disturbed. Here are the ships known to have gone down with over one hundred lives lost.
