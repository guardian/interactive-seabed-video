This area in the Java Sea was the scene of many naval battles over history, the most recent ones during the second world war.

Hundreds of ships sank during those battles and lay at the bottom of the ocean at depths reaching 80m.

In recent years many of these shipwrecks have been mysteriously salvaged for metal, and governments fear others are at risk.

Under the international salvage convention it is illegal to remove scrap without official permission. Also, many of these wreck sites are designated war graves, as thousands of dead sailors rest at the bottom of the sea.
